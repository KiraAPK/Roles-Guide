The Town's Handbook

A comprehensive guide to roles, strategy, and game flow, hosted and managed by our GameMasters.

Role Categories

Every player belongs to one of three major factions. Understanding your faction's goal is key to victory.

The Town Faction

The Town is the uninformed majority who must deduce and lynch all criminals and evildoers before it is too late.

Investigator Town (Investigative)

A private eye who secretly gathers information.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role has the ability to search a player's house, gaining a clue on their role.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Share your findings with the Town. Keep track of what you investigate and be prepared to defend your claims. You are a high-priority target for the Mafia!

Sheriff Town (Investigative)

The law enforcer of the town forced into hiding from threat of murder.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role has the ability to interrogate one player at night.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Many evils may appear to be innocent to you, or may have ways of making themselves or their allies appear innocent. Do not assume someone is a town member just because they seem to be innocent, and note that some roles can alter the results for innocent townies. Working with other investigative roles can allow you to deduce exactly what you're dealing with.

Lookout Town (Investigative)

An eagle-eyed observer, stealthily camping outside houses to gain information.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role has the ability to watch a player's home at night. The Lookout will identify up to 3 people who visit their target that night.

- The Lookout will be notified if more than 3 players visit their target that night.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

While catching a killer in the act is great, it's more important to confirm innocent townies. Confirming someone was infact where they said they were is a great way to narrow down future suspects for the town. It's common for Lookouts to visit the Jailor and other powerful town roles to confirm any town protective roles. Unlike other investigative roles, you won't draw as much suspicion to yourself for not sharing information publicly, as you can use your information to call out a liar later.

Tracker Town (Investigative)

A skilled tracker who will follow their prey to any destination.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role can track a player at night, learning who they visited that night.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Unlike the lookout, you can specifically check where one person was, but cannot confirm if other players also visited the same target. Rather than trying to catch a player in the act, it may be a good idea to track players who claim to be a role that cannot visit. There's nothing a player claiming to be a Psychic can say to draw suspicion off of themself if they get seen visiting.

Psychic Town (Investigative)

A powerful seer with a gift for finding one's secrets.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

On odd nights, the Psychic will receive the names of 3 players, one of which is guaranteed to be evil. This includes generally 'harmless' roles like Jester.

On even nights, the Psychic will receive the names of 2 players, one of which is guaranteed to be innocent. This includes Neutral Benign roles.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You excel in forcing players who may have otherwise stayed silent to share information with the town. Push your suspected players to claim, so that you may figure out who is most likely to be evil. Note that finding an evil in your odd-night vision doesn't clear the other players, nor does finding an innocent in your even-night vision make the other player evil.

Spy Town (Investigative)

A talented watcher who keeps track of evil in the Town.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role may bug a player at night.

- The bug will last until triggered.

- Bugs are triggered when something happens to the bugged player. The Spy will be notified of everything that happened to the player that night.

- Bugs can be triggered by already-present status effects.

- Some roles will always show to have a status effect.

Day Action:

The Spy can read private whispers.

Strategy & Warning:

There can only be one spy. If someone else has information only you should have, they're gaurenteed to be evil. Claiming may not be wise, as your presence encourages evildoers to be more cautious. However, your information may also be vital to the town's success. Your role is very versatile and can be played in many ways.

Doctor Town (Protective)

A surgeon skilled in trauma care who secretly heals people.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role has the ability to heal one person at night, granting them powerful defense.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Protect players who have shared useful information. If you reveal your role, the Mafia might try to trick you into healing them, or target you next. Coordinating with other protective roles can prove beneficial.

Bodyguard Town (Protective)

An ex-soldier who makes money by selling protection.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Powerful

Night Action:

This role has the ability to guard one person at night. If the Bodyguard does not visit, they will protect themselves instead.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Since you can protect yourself, you're a high-risk target for many evil roles, as killing you would kill them as well if they're not careful. Some evil roles are able to bypass your protection either by attacking indirectly or via Astral Visit. The town may not trust a Bodyguard who has lived a long time, so be ready to defend yourself.

Trapper Town (Protective)

An intelligent woodsman with a knack for traps.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Powerful

Night Action:

This role may build a trap to place on a player the following night.

- Traps take one day to build.

- Once the trap is built, they may use a night action to place it on a playerâ€™s house. They may not place it on their own house naturally. The trap will remain there until it is triggered, even if the Trapper dies.

- If the trapped player is visited, the trap will trigger and alert the trapper of all roles that visited. The trap is then used up, and the trapper must build a new one. If the trap is in an unknown location, the Trapper will still know what triggered the trap, unless the Trapper is controlled that night.

- If one of the visiting roles is an attacking role, they will be dealt a powerful attack by the trap and not attack the player. The trap may only prevent one direct attack.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Trapper is able to do more than just protect a townie. Not only do you only defend from one direct attack, other town members (or certain evil roles) can trigger your trap and waste it. You can either find a way around this or use this to your advantage to confirm other town members.

Crusader Town (Protective)

A divine protector whose skills in combat are unmatched.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role has the ability to protect a player at night, granting them powerful defense. If a player visits the target, they will be dealt a basic attack, regardless of role. The Crusader cannot attack more than one player, and cannot attack vampires.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Be careful who you protect, as you could risk killing another protective role, or even an important investigative role. However, you are a far more reliable protector than a Bodyguard, as even if you can't kill every evil, you can continue to protect a player even after you have killed their attacker.

Tavern Keeper Town (Support)

A bartender who keeps their guests distracted.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role can offer a drink to a player at night.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You cannot roleblock the same player twice, so use your ability carefully. Try to guess which mafia member may be killing on certain nights and roleblock them. Roleblocking the wrong person could be devastating to the town.

Medium Town (Support)

A secret psychic who talks with the dead.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role may speak anonymously to the dead at night.

Day Action:

Once per game, while the Medium is dead, they may choose to Seance a living player and may speak to that player the following night,

Strategy & Warning:

Make sure to put important information you learn from the dead in your Last Will! Evildoers may attempt to kill you in order to silence you. Note that the dead doesn't have to be honest.

Retributionist Town (Support)

A powerful mystic that can raise the true-hearted dead.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role can temporarily resurrect the corpse of an innocent player and command the minion to use their ability on a target of the Retributionist's choice. The corpse can be used more than once, but the Retributionist can only use corpses in the graveyard.

- The Retributionist cannot be roleblocked or controlled.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

There can only be one retributionist. If another player claims to be a retributionist, you know they're lying. Don't be afraid to risk hanging an innocent, as depending on their role, you may be able to use their ability and confirm yourself.

Monarch Town (Support)

A noble with the authority to grant knighthood.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

Twice per game, this role may knight a player, granting them an extra vote during trials. The charge is taken even if the target dies. A player elected as the Mayor cannot be knighted. If a knighted player is elected, the extra vote is removed and the charge will be returned to the Monarch.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

There can only be one Monarch. Evil roles are unlikely to want to claim to be a Monarch, but on the offchance that one does, you know they're lying. Be very careful who you grant this power! Giving the wrong player the voting power can result in chaos and destruction of the town.

Shepherd Town (Support)

A livestock guardian who's job is to alert the town.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role may choose to shout 3 times per game.

- Shouting creates an announcement the following day for 1 random player that visited the Shepherd or 2 players if they are opposing factions. The announcement doesn't show who the players visited.

- Shouts show even if the Shepherd dies, unless the Shepherd's role was hidden.

- If the Shepherd chooses to shout, they deny themselves the ability to be protected that night. Any Town Protective roles that would have visited stay home instead.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Just because several players claimed to visit you doesn't mean they're lying. Paired with a Lookout, you can confirm that several players are infact innocent. You can also play like a veteran, baiting evil roles into killing you only to expose themselves.

Vigilante Town (Killing)

A militant cop who takes the law into their own hands.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role can attack a player 3 times per game, dealing a basic attack to their target. The Vigilante cannot do this night 1, unless controlled.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Do not shoot randomly! If you shoot a Town member, you will die of guilt the next night. Only shoot players who are confirmed evil (by an Investigator or other reliable source).

Veteran Town (Killing)

A paranoid war hero who will shoot anyone who visits them.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Powerful

Night Action:

This role can go on alert 3 times per game, gaining basic defense and dealing a powerful attack to all visitors.

- The Veteran cannot be roleblocked or controlled.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

There can only be one Veteran. If another player claims to be a Veteran, you know they're lying. Evil players are wary of a veteran. Try to get evil players to visit you, but don't be too obvious, or they will suspect a trap. Be careful not to accidentally kill innocent townies in the process!

Jailor Town (Killing)

A prison guard who secretly detains suspects.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Powerful

Night Action:

The Jailor may talk anonymously with the jailed player. The player will be unable to talk in any team private chats.

- The Jailor may choose to execute the player three times per game. The Jailor may not do this on Night 1. Being roleblocked stops execution.

- If the Jailor executes an innocent player, the Jailor will lose the ability to jail.

- Failing to execute a dangerous role, usually Serial Killer, will result in the Jailor's death.

Day Action:

This role may choose one person during the day to haul off to jail the following night.

Strategy & Warning:

There can only be one Jailor, but evil players will almost never claim to be a Jailor. You can use your ability to get a claim out of silent players, or execute a player who you are confident is evil. Executing neutrals is rarely a bad thing, as neutrals will often turn against the town. It is however looked down upon to execute a neutral who has not threatened the town.

Trickster Town (Killing)

A mischevious magician who uses mirrors for their tricks.

Goal:

Lynch every criminal and evildoer.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role may place a mirror at someoneâ€™s house at night, absorbing one direct attack. The Trickster will know what attack they absorbed.

- The Trickster may target themself once per game.

- Mirrors function as a normal protect and only last one night.

- The Trickster may use any absorbed attacks on a player of their choice, and recieves no punishment for killing an innocent.

- Attacks show as whatever attack it absorbed and used.

- The Trickster may not use both abilities at the same time.

- The Trickster CAN be killed by Bodyguard, Trapper, etc.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

While you recieve no punishment for killing an innocent, you should still be careful, as accidentally killing an innocent could result in the Town's loss, and you'll have to absorb another attack to kill again. Otherwise, you're essentially a protective role with a gun.

The Mafia Faction

The Mafia wins when everyone who would oppose them is dead. They operate in a private chat at night and have abilities designed for deception and coordination.

Ambusher Mafia (Killing)

A stealthy killer who lies in wait for the perfect moment to strike.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role has the ability to prepare an ambush outside of a player's house, attacking one non-mafia visitor. All visitors will learn who the Ambusher is.

There may only be one Ambusher.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

Try to take out protective or investigative roles early, before the town has the chance to coordinate. Be careful not to visit someone who may have a lot of visitors, as you can only kill one, but all of them will learn your name.

Demolitionist Mafia (Killing)

An artificer who uses their skills for terrorism.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

Twice per game, this role may visit another non-Mafia player at night to pass them a bomb.

- The player, upon recieving the bomb, may now pass it to another player via visiting them. This fails if the Demolitionist detonates the bomb that day or night, or if the target is protected by a Guardian Angel or Admirer. The bomb will be passed at the end of the night/day.

- At night, the player will pass the bomb to whoever they use their ability on. Roles that pick two targets may pick which target to pass the bomb to. Non-visiting roles gain the ability to visit in order to pass the bomb.

- There may only be one bomb present at a time. The Demolitionist will know who currently has the bomb.

- There may only be one Demolitionist.

Day Action:

After one day, at any time, the Demolitionist may choose to detonate the bomb, which will explode at the end of the day/night with a warning.

Strategy & Warning:

The town will often attempt to pick someone to be killed by the bomb. The bomb CAN be passed to a mafia member by any non-mafia player, which will allow you to coordinate exactly who you want to kill. Try to manipulate the town into placing the bomb where you'd like, or use the chaos you cause to your advantage. Be careful who you initially pass the bomb to, as if the player the bomb is passed to dies the same night, the bomb will be gone.

Kidnapper Mafia (Killing)

A corrupt prison guard who detains people in their basement.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Powerful

Night Action:

The Kidnapper may talk anonymously with a kidnapped player, and will appear to be a Jailor. The player will be unable to talk in any team private chats. ALL messages sent will be seen by both the mafia and the kidnapped player.

- The Kidnapper may choose to exterminate the player once per game. The death will appear to have been from a Jailor.

- The Kidnapper cannot jail the Jailor, or any player elected as Mayor, no matter their role. However, the Kidnapper can be jailed.

- There can only be one Kidnapper..

Day Action:

This role may choose one person during the day to kidnap the following night. The kidnapped player will not be able to preform any night actions. The Kidnapper may only do this three times.

- The Kidnapper cannot kidnap fellow mafia members.

Strategy & Warning:

In most cases, pretending to be the jailor during the day is a bad idea, as the real jailor can and will execute you. Even if there is no jailor, you do not have as much capability as a jailor, and the town will likely notice your lack of action. Instead, when you kidnap someone, use your anonymity to attempt to gain extra information that the mafia would not normally have. You're better off using your execute as simply an extra kill for the mafia rather than a deceptive play. (KIDNAPPER SHOULD BE PLAYED IN VERY DIFFERENT WAYS DEPENDING ON THE GAMEMODE!)

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Janitor Mafia (Deception)

A master of disguise who can make people appear to be something they are not.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role can clean a player's house at night. The Janitor can only do this 3 times. If the player dies, their role and last will is hidden.

Day Action:

The Janitor will be informed of the player's role and last will.

Strategy & Warning:

Your best use is suppressing information. While you usually don't have to think about who to clean (just clean the kill volunteer's target, duh) you SHOULD be careful when you do so. If the volunteer fails to kill the target, your ability will be wasted, or you could even risk your own death if the target happens to be a veteran, or the target of a rampaging Neutral Killing role like Werewolf. To cause more confusion, you can try to predict who other evil roles will attempt to kill, albeit this method is less reliable. Claiming to be the role you cleaned isn't always a good idea, as you might not be able to keep up the fakeclaim, or the player's role may have already been known and you will be called out.

Forger Mafia (Deception)

A crooked lawyer that replaces documents.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role can forge a player's documents. If the player dies that night, their shown role/last will is replaced with what the Forger selected. The Forger can do this 2 times.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You are NOT just a worse janitor, and should not use your ability as such! You're capable of setting yourself or your teammates up for the perfect fakeclaim, or incriminating an innocent town member without putting yourself at risk. Forging someone who has already claimed is not usually wise, though you can alter their will to say something that benefits you. Note that if there's a medium, the dead chat will likely call out any obvious patterns!

Hypnotist Mafia (Deception)

A skilled hypnotist who can alter the perception of others.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role can plant a memory on another player, giving them a false notification of the Hypnotist's choosing.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You can mimic ANY possible notification, but your actions do not overwrite any real notifications. An investigative role will know they weren't actually roleblocked if they successfully gained information, for example. Use your ability wisely to either sow confusion or back up your teammates' (or even your own) claims, but be aware that a single contradiction in notifications can expose the entire plan.

Godfather Mafia (Support)

The leader of organized crime.

Basic

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

When choosing to kill, the Godfather takes priority over the other mafia.

Day Action:

This role may send a private message to another mafia member during the day, twice per day. There will be no public notification, and nobody else will be able to read the message.

Strategy & Warning:

The Sheriff will find you Innocent unless you have killed that night. You can use this as a means to gain the town's trust. Use your private messages to lead your teammates without drawing suspicion to yourself or risk your whispers being overheard.

If you use your information to make yourself trustworthy to the town, be careful what you claim and what you share! Lookouts only see who visits a player, and Trackers only see who a player visits. Mixing these up could get you hanged!

Strategy & Warning:

You are the Mafia's most reliable source of information! Be sure to share your info with the mafia, as even if you've shared some info publicly, you can't rely on your teammates to have seen or remember it. It's reccomended to act as an advisor to the mafia. Just like the real deal, hehe

Moonshiner Mafia (Support)

A smuggler who illegally sells alcohol.

Goal:

Kill anyone who will not submit to the Mafia.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

This role can offer a drink to a player at night. The player will be too drunk to perform any actions and be forced to remain home.

- The player will not get hungover from a Moonshiner roleblock.

- Once per game, the Moonshiner may add poison to a drink. Poison is a direct basic attack that takes a day to kill.

- The Moonshiner cannot be roleblocked.

- There can only be one Moonshiner.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You ignore hangovers and cannot apply Hangover. Be careful not to overlap with tavernkeepers, or risk outing yourself! Alternatively, if you don't care if the town knows you exist, you can choose to shut down an important town member like the Jailor, though they may get a Lookout or Crusader to deal with you, or a Werewolf/similar could take the opportunity for a doublekill.

The Neutral Faction

Neutrals have diverse win conditions. They prioritize their own victory, often at the expense of others.

Amnesiac Neutral (Benign)

A trauma patient that does not remember who they were.

Goal:

Remember who you were and complete that role's goal.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role has the ability to become another role from the graveyard. They will not actually become the role until the day starts.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

It is publicly announced when Amnesiac remembers a role. Claiming to be the Amnesiac may make you the target of Town's lynching if you or another Amnesiac remembers an evil role. Other players may also threaten to kill you if you do not help them. Be careful who you trust.

Guardian Angel Neutral (Benign)

An angel whose only goal is the protection of their charge.

Goal:

Keep your target alive until the end of the game.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

The Guardian Angel can protect their target twice per game, granting their target powerful defense, preventing them from being executed during the day, and purging them of any dangerous effects (e.g., being doused). The Guardian Angel can do this while dead.

- There can only be one Guardian Angel.

Day Action:

This role is automatically assigned a target that they must keep alive for the entire game. The Guardian Angel cannot have a Jester or Executioner as their target. If the Guardian Angel's target dies, the Guardian Angel becomes an Amnesiac.

Strategy & Warning:

Do not assume your target can handle themself. You're a glorified babysitter. Imagine that your target dying is like losing track of the kid you're supposed to be babysitting. That's it, that's the tip.

Admirer Neutral (Benign)

A fucking simp.

Goal:

Marry someone and keep them alive until the end of the game.

Defense Level:

None

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

The Admirer can protect their target twice per game, granting them powerful defense and purging them of any dangerous effects. Admirer cannot do this the night they propose.

Day Action:

Twice per game, after Night 1, this role may choose to propose to a player during the day. They will propose the following night. If the player accepts, the Admirer will become bound to them, learning their role, and can only win with them. If the Admirer's lover dies, the Admirer also dies. The Admirer can win while dead, as long as their lover is alive.

Strategy & Warning:

Similar to an Amnesiac, claiming to be an Admirer may result in you being threatened into siding with a specific faction. However, once you've already proposed to someone, there's not much anyone else can do. You can generally do whatever you want, as long as you're careful who you pick. Try to side with who's most likely to win!

Witch Neutral (Evil)

A voodoo master who can control other peoples' actions.

Goal:

Live to see Town lose the game.

Defense Level:

Basic (Barrier)

Attack Power:

None

Night Action:

This role can control a player each night, forcing their victim to use their ability on a player of the Witch's choosing.

Day Action:

None

Strategy & Warning:

You win with everyone but town, so making alliances with other players is your key to success. Use your ability to make openings for evil roles to kill off important town members, or to sow chaos by using the town's own killing power against them. Be aware that other players may not trust you, especially if rival evil factions exist, and that other players may betray you to save themselves.

More Neutral Evil roles (Executioner) would be listed here using similar cards.

Serial Killer Neutral (Killing)

A psychotic criminal who wants everyone to die..

Goal:

Kill everyone who opposes you.

Defense Level:

Basic

Attack Power:

Basic

Night Action:

- This role can kill one player each night.

- Every time the Serial Killer successfully kills a player, they gain Bloodlust.

- After 2 stacks of Bloodlust, the Serial killer's next attack will be powerful, and the Serial Killer will also attack all players who visit their target.

- The Serial Killer keeps these boosts until they fail to kill someone.

- If the Serial Killer fails to kill, their Bloodlust is reset. The failed kill is counted before any others.

- If the Serial Killer attacks another Serial Killer, both players will know each other's roles and be able to talk to each other at night.

- The Serial Killer will automatically kill anyone that roleblocks them. However, the Serial Killer may choose to be cautious and not kill roleblockers, as well as appearing innocent to the Sheriff, but will as a result lose their roleblock immunity and all bloodlust, and cannot gain more until Cautious is disabled.

Game Phases & Flow

The game alternates between the Night and Day phases until a winning condition is met.

Understanding the cycle is essential for strategic timing.

Night Phase (Action)

  • Actions: All roles with night abilities perform their actions (killing, investigating, healing, protecting). All night actions are submitted privately to the GameMaster. Some abilities are used during the day instead.
  • Communication: Only Mafia and select Neutral roles (such as Vampires) may communicate with their team in their private chat channels. Town members cannot communicate at night unless it is between a Jailor and their target.

Day Phase (Discussion & Voting)

  • Morning: The previous night's events and deaths are announced by the GameMaster.
  • Discussion: Players argue, claim roles, and try to find contradictions in testimonies.
  • Voting: Players nominate someone for trial. If enough votes are secured, the accused faces the tribunal. Day Actions (like the Jailor selecting who to jail for the night) are typically used during this phase.
  • Election: On Day 3 specifically, the Discussion phase is replaced with the Election phase. During this phase, players may elect a Mayor. Any role can be the Mayor, so players must choose wisely. The mayor's vote counts for 3 instead of 1.

Trial & Judgement

  • Defense: The accused player pleads their case.
  • Judgement: The Town votes 'Guilty', 'Innocent', or 'Abstain'. A 'Guilty' verdict results in immediate execution and the day ending early, while an 'Innocent' verdict or a tie vote results in the voting phase continuing. There cannot be more than 3 trials in one day.

Mastering the Game

For Town Players

  • Communicate with GM: If your role allows private communication, follow the GameMaster's instructions on how to submit your action or message a trusted player.
  • Claim Early, but Wisely: Investigative roles (like Investigator or Sheriff) should claim early, as they are the Town's main source of information, but be aware the Mafia will target you. Not providing the town with important information may draw suspicion on you.
  • Gather Information: Using trials to question quiet players is a great way to narrow down suspects faster, but be aware that someone who claims a role may be lying. Some players may claim early to avoid being put on trial later.
  • Keep Logs: Last Wills are a great tool for sharing your concerns even after you die. Since town members usually have little to hide, it is common for them to contain their notes within their Last Will instead of separately. It's also important to log your night actions to be looked back on later for potential contradictions.

For Mafia Players

  • Form a Will: Swiftly decide on a believable Town role (like Sheriff) and write a fake will/log to post in the event of your death. Town members may ask to see your Last Will, but note that you don't have to share the whole thing, only logs of your 'Night Actions.'
  • Vote with Conviction: Do not abstain from voting. Voting 'Innocent' for a fellow Mafia member is expected. Voting 'Guilty' on an innocent Townie may also draw suspicion if you had no reason to do so, but Abstaining may also draw suspicion on you as players may suspect you are being passive on purpose.
  • Know the Claims: Keep track of who has claimed which Town role, as to better choose targets at night while not accidentally contradicting them during the day.
  • Coordination: Mafia thrives on coordination and teamwork. Use your ability wisely and plan ahead with your teammates at night.

Status Effects

  • Roleblocked: Roleblocked players cannot preform an action that night. The player will know they have been roleblocked and usually will know what roleblocked them. Some roles are indistinguishable from other roles in this matter. Certain roles cannot be roleblocked under certain circumstances.
  • Blackmailed: Blackmailed players are unable to speak or whisper during the day. They may still vote, read the chat and be whispered to. If put on trial, they may state that they were blackmailed and cannot speak, but nothing else.
  • Poisoned: Poisoned players will die at the end of the night if not healed by a Doctor or Potion Master. This counts as a Basic Attack.
  • Framed: A framed player will appear as a different (usually evil) role to investigative roles. The player will not know they have been framed.
  • Doused: A doused player will appear as an Arsonist to certain investigative roles (usually those that specifically check for role). If any arsonist chooses to ignite, all doused players will die, even if doused by a different arsonist. Players will not know they have been doused, unless they are an Arsonist.
  • Infected: Infected players can infect other players, either by visiting them or being visited by them. This status effect has no effect, but may still be seen by the Spy.
  • Posessed: A posessed player is unable to speak, vote or whisper. The Kitsune is able to speak using their voice, but cannot whisper using them.
  • Hangover: A player that has been roleblocked by a Tavern Keeper cannot be roleblocked by the same or another Tavern Keeper the following night.

Common Interactions/Gimmicks

  • Some roles have temporary defense, called Barriers. These roles will have basic defense until they are attacked. If they are attacked again afterwards, they will die. An evil role attacking a role with a barrier will display a unique Defense message. The same message will display for protections preformed by non-town roles.
  • Certain roles are Roleblock Immune and cannot be roleblocked by a Tavern Keeper, Moonshiner or Auditor. Roleblockers will be unaware if their target was immune to being roleblocked. The roles that are Roleblock Immune are: Tavern Keeper, Retributionist, Veteran, Moonshiner, Auditor, Changeling (N1), Serial Killer (unless cautious), Pirate, and Witch.
  • Basic Defense beats Basic Attack, Powerful Defense beats Powerful Attack, and Invincible Defense beats Unstoppable Attack.

Uncommon Interactions/Gimmicks

  • A player protected by a Guardian Angel cannot be put on trial that day.
  • If a Godfather is controlled, they will be forced to kill and override the Mafia's Volunteer.
  • A Changeling that takes over a Mafia role cannot volunteer to kill, even if they replaced the Godfather.
  • Changeling cannot use the Godfather's ability.
  • Changeling does not count towards the role limit of the role they replace.
